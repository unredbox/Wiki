Overview

These units were installed on Outside units that were installed in 2005 after the Indoor units were found to be successful at a few test locations in 2004 under kiosks that were formally DVDPlay kiosks.

Air Conditioner

These machines were installed and used the a modified version of the IceQube IQ2200VS Air Conditioners. All manuals/parts are available on the IceQube website and replacement parts have to be ordered over the phone. Expect the parts to be a bit pricey. Cheapest parts would to find used parts in another unit, but the guarantee of said parts to last is close to none.

Recall Notices

All units manufactured before 2012 had a recall with the Blower Motors, which could become a fire hazard. If your unit was manufactured before 2012 and the Blower Motors parts BLWR1011-A and BLWR1007-A that are older than 2012, you will need to order replacement parts from IceQube before powering these units.

IQ2200VS Programming

To enter programming mode on the IceQube IQ2200VS, use the password 1234 (note: some blue units may use 1234321). If the password is accepted, the lights will indicate the current settings. Use buttons 1 and 2 to adjust values, and button 3 to move to the next section. When finished, press button 4 to exit programming mode. All changes made will apply to that specific controller.

If you tried both passwords and you are unable to get into programming mode, you will need to contact IceQube or swap the controller out from another Kiosk's Unit or order the part.

IQ2200VS Troubleshooting

Always unplug the unit before troubleshooting or performing any maintenance to prevent electric shock. Even when unplugged, discharge any capacitors before touching internal components. Never attempt to bypass or modify the plug or internal wiring while the unit is connected to power.

3.10, 3.12, 3.14

Indicates a shorted sensor. 

Solution:
Inspect the sensor cable for damage. If damaged, a replacement is required to clear this code.

3.15, 3.16

Indicates supply voltage is either too high or too low.

Solution:
Check power supply for high or low voltage condition. May indicate damage caused by power spike. If this is the case, a replacement of the unit is needed.

EC

Indicates a shorted condenser sensor. 

Solution:
Inspect the sensor cable for damage. If damaged, a replacement is required to clear this code.

EO

Indicates an open condenser sensor.

Solution:
Inspect the sensor cable for damage and check that it is plugged into the rear of the controller. If plugged and still occurs, you will need to replace the condenser.

212

Indicates a shorted evaporator sensor. 

Solution:
Inspect the sensor cable for damage. If damaged, a replacement is required to clear this code.

CC

Indicates a shorted condenser sensor.

Solution:
Inspect the sensor cable for damage. If damaged, a replacement is required to clear this code.

C-O

Indicates an open condenser sensor.

Solution:
Inspect the sensor cable for damage and check that it is plugged into the rear of the controller. If damaged, a replacement is required to clear this code.

C-C

Indicates a shorted condenser sensor.

Solution:
Inspect the sensor cable for damage. If damaged, a replacement is required to clear this code.

Blank Display

Indicates loss of power to the controller or a defective controller. 

Solution:
Check the output voltage at the transformer secondary. The voltage should be a nominal 24 VAC. If the proper voltage is available at the transformer and the controller harness is secure to the controller, the controller is most likely faulty and will need replacement.

Flashing Cool LED

Indicates that the cooling has not been initiated. Within 3.5 minutes, the Cool LED should be lit continuously, and cooling should begin. When the Cool LED is lit continuously, the compressor and condenser blower should be operating.

PDCM IQPDCM-15

These were installed in all kiosks, even if the AC units weren't recalled. These served when to prevent the Air Conditioners from catching on fire, but upon notice from Former Employees (FSRs and Techs), these have been known to run poorly or outright fail leaving a burn mark on the box or the box catching fire in rare cases. Most recommend unplugging this from the AC unit and toss it out.

IQ2200VS Additional Information

You can find more info and manuals on IceQube's official website under VS Series Air Conditioners.

Air Exchanger

These machines were installed and used the IceQube IQ900FFS Air Exchangers. There are no manuals/known replacement parts for these kiosks as they are proprietary to Redbox and was manufactured under IceQube.

IQ900FFS Programming

You can turn these units on and off through Field Maintenance or HAL Tester. If its not configured, you will need to configure this in HAL Tester in order for the Air Exchanger button to appear in Field Maintenance.

At this moment in time, there is no documented way to adjust the temperature besides disabling the alarm inside of the kiosk.

IQ900FFS Additional Information

At this time, there is no manuals or parts list as its proprietary to Redbox. Likely over time when parts start failing or error codes appear in HAL Tester, there will be more information added on.

Note that the filters will need to be remained clean or else the dust can start flowing around the kiosk and can damage kiosk equipment and discs. If the filter gets dusty, with the stock filter, you will need to spray it out with a hose (do not power wash as they will become damaged). If you need to replace these, you can purchase standard Aluminum Mesh Filters (which can be washed and reused)
